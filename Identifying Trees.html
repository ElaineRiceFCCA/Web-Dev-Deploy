Identifying Trees

The identification of plants can seem a formidable task when you are just beginning. The basic rule being, if a botanist has no idea what a particular plant is, they will use a key to identify it. In practice you can use a book for identification, however, you need to know what to look for. Leaves are often good characters, in particulate their size, shape and colour, but the most reliable guides are the flowers and fruit. However, in winter you may not have leaves or flowers available and therefore you must rely on bark or twig characteristics.
