Rowan Tree

Rowan trees bring vibrant hues to woodlands across Ireland, particularly in elevated regions where they flourish even amidst rocky terrain, earning them the nickname "mountain ash".

Their creamy blossoms mature into scarlet berries early in the season, offering sustenance to thrushes throughout the winter months.

During winter, a mistle thrush will fiercely defend a rowan tree or holly, not for nesting purposes but to safeguard its feeding territory.

Rowan trees are prized additions to gardens, thriving in well-drained sites while exhibiting adaptability to various soil types.

Different components of the rowan tree, such as its bark, berries, and leaves, have been utilized in traditional medicine. Rich in vitamin C, the berries have been crafted into jams, jellies, and alcoholic beverages. Additionally, the bark and leaves were historically employed in treating ailments like diarrhea, sore throats, and fevers.
