Strawberry Tree

The Arbutus, commonly known as the strawberry tree, is a compact evergreen tree that can grow to towering heights of up to 15 meters. Its distribution is distinctive, being naturally found in the Mediterranean region and select areas of Ireland.

Unlike many native trees that arrived in Ireland via Great Britain, the Arbutus is believed to have migrated here across the land bridge from Brittany.

Named for its fruit's unique shape and color, the strawberry tree predominates in County Kerry, particularly in the Killarney district, where it forms a significant part of the natural forest on the islands and shores of the lakes. It also thrives in unshaded areas of Glengarriff Wood, County Cork, and around Lough Gill in County Sligo.

In November and December, the Arbutus bursts into bloom with profuse white flowers. Due to the fruit's year-long ripening process, the tree often bears both mature fruit and flowers simultaneously.

While the fruit is technically edible, its Latin name is Unedo, meaning "I eat one" implying it is not particularly enjoyable.
