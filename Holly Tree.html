Holly Tree

The native evergreen holly contributes to the shrub layer in our oldest woodlands, thriving in environments such as narrow gullies in the Sperrins or Donegal uplands, where it endures harsh conditions alongside rowan trees.

Renowned for its visual appeal, holly is a popular choice for gardens, serving as an eye-catching specimen tree or dense hedge, albeit slow-growing.

Holly trees exhibit sexual dimorphism, with only females bearing the iconic berries, making it advisable to plant multiple trees for optimal fruiting. Both male and female trees produce small creamy flowers.

Despite shedding its spiny leaves year-round, particularly in summer heat, holly remains green throughout the year, historically prized alongside ivy for Christmas adornments, symbolizing life amidst winter's dormancy.

In some traditions, cutting down holly is deemed unlucky, leading to its preservation as hedgerows.
